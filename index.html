Soft-Body Physics

Unlike traditional racing games, BeamNG.drive uses real-time soft-body physics. Every vehicle component is simulated individually, creating the most realistic damage and deformation ever seen in a driving simulator.

Nodes & Beams

Vehicles are constructed from interconnected nodes (mass points) and beams (spring-damper connections), creating a physics skeleton that deforms realistically under stress.

Real-Time Deformation

Body panels crumple, glass shatters, and suspension components break under impact. No pre-canned animations—everything is calculated in real-time.

Powertrain Simulation

Engines, transmissions, and differentials are fully simulated with realistic friction, inertia, and thermal properties. Clutch slip, gear wear, and engine damage all affect performance.

Tire Model

Advanced tire physics simulate slip angle, load sensitivity, temperature buildup, and wear patterns. Different compounds behave realistically on various surfaces.
