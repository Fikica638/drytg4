Why BeamNG?

A decade of development has created the most sophisticated vehicle simulation engine in gaming history.

Real-Time Physics

Every vehicle component is physically simulated. Body panels crumple, suspension compresses, and engines overheat based on real-world physics calculations.

Authentic Damage

No pre-canned crash animations. Every dent, scratch, and structural failure is calculated in real-time based on impact forces and material properties.

Massive Open World

Drive across sprawling 16x16 km maps featuring diverse biomes, detailed road networks, and dynamic terrain deformation for off-road adventures.

Deep Customization

Swap engines, upgrade turbos, adjust suspension geometry, and tune differentials. Every vehicle has thousands of configurative possibilities.

Multiplayer

Modding Support

Proprietary Technology

Soft-Body Physics

Unlike traditional racing games using rigid bodies, BeamNG.drive utilizes a custom-built soft-body physics engine. Every vehicle is simulated as a network of interconnected nodes and beams, calculating forces in real-time at 2000Hz.

Nodes & Beams Structure

Vehicles are constructed from mass points (nodes) connected by spring-damper pairs (beams). This skeleton deforms realistically under impact, accurately transferring forces throughout the chassis.

Real-Time Deformation

Body panels crumple based on material thickness and yield strength. Glass shatters using Voronoi fracture patterns. Suspension components bend and break when overstressed. No two crashes are identical.

Powertrain Simulation

Engines feature realistic friction, pumping losses, and thermal dynamics. Transmissions model gear lash, synchromesh wear, and clutch slip. Differentials accurately distribute torque based on traction.

Advanced Tire Model

Pacejka-based tire model simulates slip angle, load sensitivity, camber thrust, and temperature buildup. Different compounds offer trade-offs between grip and longevity on various surfaces.

Important Performance Notice

BeamNG.drive is extremely CPU-intensive due to real-time soft-body physics calculations. Performance scales heavily with single-threaded CPU speed and core count. For multiplayer servers or large-scale crash scenarios with 8+ vehicles, a high-end modern CPU (Ryzen 7/9 or Intel i7/i9) with 32GB+ RAM is strongly recommended. Storage speed affects map loading times significantly; an NVMe SSD is highly recommended.
